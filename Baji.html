Baji

Baji is a type of rice that can be eaten in Nepal. This and the most common word commonly encountered by visitors to Nepal is "Chiura". "Baji" is a term used in the Newar culture of the Kathmandu Valley. Rice is a staple of Nepal, as it is throughout South Asia. Grains are integrated into everyday life, festivals and rituals. Chiura is a popular treat among rural farmers and workers in Nepal. Snacks are made by tapping rice. The dish can be served with yogurt, curry and meat. Chiura is often paired with tea as a morning treat in the fields.

Chiura holds a traditional Nepal wedding important place. Kiura is usually included in the bride bridal family valuables or a rich bride award. After the first wedding of the wedding, the family came back to the groom at the groom. During this matrix, a baked ceremonial ritual occurs. The bride and groom sit next to each other and the bride receives Chiura. The groom then formally asks the bride to give Chiura. He asks this two more times. Each time, she uses the "you" form to indicate her decline in social status. This ceremony confirms the fact that a woman's social status is far below her original status. Chiura's popular simple snacks are Chiurako Dakani and Chiurako Prao.

Chiura Dakani

Ingredients

Chiura (beaten rice), ghee (clarified butter), milk and sugar.

Procedure

First, heat the pan and add ghee. Once the ghee has melted, add dry chiura and fry. When fully fried (looks crunchy), add warm milk and sugar (if you like). Mix the ingredients properly and when the milk evaporates, stir well to remove from heat. Ready to serve.

Chiura ko Pulao

Ingredients

Chiura ,chopped carrot, chopped cabbage, chopped cauliflower, chopped tomatoes, shelled green peas, chopped green capsicum, fenugreek seeds, cumin seeds, thyme seeds, bay leaf, turmeric, oil for cooking, ghee (optional), salt to taste and chopped coriander leaves.

Procedure

First of all, heat a pan and add oil to it. When the oil is heated, splatter fenugreek, cumin, thyme and bay leaf. When the seeds start to crackle, add chopped carrot, cabbage, cauliflower, green peas and capsicum and stir fry it for about five minutes.

In a bowl, wash the chiura and drain the water. Add the washed chiura into the vegetable mixture and stir fry it for next five minutes. You could add ghee if you desire. When it is ready, add some chopped coriander leaves and serve it hot.
