Culture

The Newar are one of many ethnic groups in Nepal and are considered to be the indigenous people of the Kathmandu Valley. Experience the best of Newar culture, architecture, food and festivals on this culturally rich 7-day tour. Newar culture is found in the three ancient kingdoms of the valleys of Kathmandu, Pattern and Bhaktapur. These kingdoms are well known on this fulfilling tour.

The Newar have a unique lifestyle and are very culturally rich. The different ways of living and celebrating the festival unite the Newar and bring a sense of brotherhood and cooperation. From birth to death, there are a variety of unique customs and family gatherings that enrich the Newar culture. In fact, the Newar culture is very broad and consists of many castes with different cultures, traditions and unique values. The Newar are believed to be the first inhabitants of the Kathmandu Valley, but their origin is a mystery. Their language is Newar. They were happy to celebrate various festivals and customs.

Pyakha

NEWARI Culture was a TAD bit, not for masked dances. The complexity of New Yo culture, wealth, multilayer aspects are revealed by their bright festivals and their lush art, but they have been clarified from its wonderful masked dances called "Pyakha". `In the Newari/NewA language, the term "pyakha" can specify either drama or dance. The mask forms an essential center of all pyakha performance.

The mask that covers the performer's face is called Kwapa, and interestingly, it means "Mask" in Newar. Previously, masks were usually made by members of the local painter Chitrakar caste. In many parts of Bhaktapur, the Shakya Newar made and painted their own masks, but in Bhaktapur, the Potter-class "Jap" Newar made the masks.

Newari Music

Newa music, also known as Newar music, is a traditional music developed by Newars in Nepal. Music has its roots in classical Hindu and Buddhist music. It was developed by incorporating folk songs in and around the Kathmandu Valley. Musical instruments mainly consist of percussion and wind instruments.

Traditional music points to the 16th century music during the century of the first 10th century.
